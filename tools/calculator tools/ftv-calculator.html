Frequently Asked Questions

Future Time Value (FTV) is a financial concept that calculates how much your current money will be worth in the future, considering factors like interest rates, compounding frequency, and time. It's crucial for financial planning as it helps you understand the growth potential of your investments and make informed decisions about saving and investing. This concept is fundamental to retirement planning, investment analysis, and general financial goal-setting.

Compounding frequency determines how often interest is calculated and added to your principal amount. The more frequent the compounding (e.g., monthly vs. annually), the higher your returns will be. For example, if you have $10,000 invested at 5% annual interest, you'll earn more with monthly compounding than with annual compounding because you're earning interest on your interest more frequently.

Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time. By including inflation in your calculations, you get a more realistic view of your investment's future value in terms of today's purchasing power. For example, if your investment grows by 6% annually but inflation is 2%, your real return (inflation-adjusted) is only 4%. Our calculator helps you understand this important distinction.

Regular contributions can significantly boost your investment's future value through dollar-cost averaging and compound interest. For example, adding just $100 monthly to an initial $10,000 investment at 6% annual interest can result in tens of thousands more over a 20-year period compared to the initial investment alone. Our calculator shows you exactly how much impact your regular contributions will have.

Our calculator uses standard financial formulas and provides highly accurate results. However, remember that future values are projections based on your inputs and assumptions about interest rates, inflation, and market conditions. While the mathematical calculations are precise, actual returns may vary due to market fluctuations, changing interest rates, and other external factors. We recommend using this tool for planning purposes and consulting with a financial advisor for specific investment advice.
